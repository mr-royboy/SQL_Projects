# Business Insights

## Revenue Insights
Revenue analysis shows how sales change over time. Tracking monthly and yearly revenue helps identify periods of strong performance and possible seasonal trends.

Month-over-month revenue growth provides insight into how the business is expanding and helps detect sudden increases or decreases in sales.

## Customer Insights
Customer spending analysis reveals that a small number of customers generate a significant portion of total revenue. These high-value customers are important for retention and targeted marketing strategies.

Analyzing orders per customer helps understand purchasing behavior and customer engagement levels.

City-level revenue analysis highlights which locations generate the most sales, helping businesses identify strong markets.

## Product Performance
Product-level analysis identifies which products generate the highest revenue.

Category-level analysis helps determine which product groups contribute most to overall sales.

Identifying the top-performing product within each category helps businesses focus on promoting successful items and optimizing inventory.

## Order Behavior
Average order value indicates how much customers typically spend in a single purchase.

Analyzing payment methods helps identify the most preferred payment option among customers.

Cancelled order rate provides insight into potential operational or customer experience issues that may affect order completion.

## Return Analysis
Return rate per product helps identify products that customers frequently return, which may indicate quality issues or mismatched customer expectations.

Analyzing returns by category helps detect problem areas within the product catalog.

Refund amount trends show how product returns impact the company's revenue over time.

## Strategic Insights
This analysis helps businesses understand customer behavior, identify top-performing products, monitor sales trends, and detect potential issues related to product returns.
